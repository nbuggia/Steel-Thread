Rewrite the first paragraph to hook the reader. The opening is the only part every reader reads — if it doesn't earn their attention, the rest doesn't matter.

## Steps

**1. Read CONTEXT.md**
Understand who the reader is and what they care about. The hook must be calibrated to this specific audience, not a general reader.

**2. Read the document**
Read the full document — not just the opening. The hook should accurately represent what follows. A misleading hook is worse than a slow one.

**3. Diagnose the current opening**
Name the problem specifically before rewriting:
- Starts with context the reader already has
- Leads with methodology instead of finding
- Opens with "This document will..." or "In this report..."
- First sentence could belong to any document on this topic
- Buries the most interesting thing three paragraphs in

**4. Write three alternatives**

Each alternative should:
- Open with the most compelling claim or finding
- Be specific — not "this changes everything" but "X reduced Y by 40% in three months"
- Match the voice defined in CONTEXT.md
- Be one paragraph maximum

**5. Present the alternatives**

Show all three. For each: name the hook strategy ("leads with the finding", "leads with the reader's problem", "leads with the contrast").

Ask: "Which direction feels right, or is there a different approach you want to try?"

**6. Apply the customer's choice**

Write the chosen version into the document.

## Rules

- Never write a generic hook that could belong to any document
- The opening must be accurate — do not promise what the document doesn't deliver
- If the document has no compelling hook anywhere in it, say so: "The most interesting thing I found is in paragraph 4. Want to move it to the front?"
